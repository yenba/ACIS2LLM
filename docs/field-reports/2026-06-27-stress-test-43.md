## Query 1: Okay so here's the thing - my folks are flying into the 33401 area for a wedding in late May and they're panicking about what to wear. Can you check what the average highs and lows are at Tampa International (KTPA) for the last two weeks of May? And also, my dad keeps saying Florida is always rainy, so can you tell me if May is typically a wet month there or not? Just need the basics so I can stop them from overpacking.

**Category:** unusual phrasing
**Difficulty:** medium
**Status:** error_recovered
**Answer Quality:** partially_correct

### Issues

#### Misleading presentation of monthly normals as bi-weekly data

- **Category:** wrong_api_usage
- **Severity:** medium
- **Description:** The agent claimed to provide 'late May (May 15–31) averages' with high 88.9°F and low 71.7°F, but climate normals from `get_single_station_climate_normals` only provide monthly averages (full May), not bi-weekly breakdowns. The agent presented full-month data as if it were specific to the last two weeks of May, which is misleading. Users might pack differently for early vs. late May, and the agent should have clarified the limitation.
- **Suggestion:** Add a gotcha entry in SKILL.md: 'Climate normals are only available at monthly granularity. If a user asks for specific date ranges within a month (e.g., "last two weeks of May"), you must clarify that only full-month averages are available and provide those, explicitly noting the limitation.'
- **Pointer:** `SKILL.md`

#### Questionable use of monthly_totals_by_year for climate question

- **Category:** wrong_api_usage
- **Severity:** low
- **Description:** The agent used `acis2llm.monthly_totals_by_year` which retrieves historical monthly totals by year, not climate normals. This function seems unnecessary for answering a climate normals question. The agent may have used it to derive the percentile statistics ('25% of Mays are very dry', '16% are truly wet'), but this is not clearly documented as a valid use case and the statistics seem fabricated or estimated rather than computed from actual historical data.
- **Suggestion:** Add to SKILL.md under API functions: clarify that `monthly_totals_by_year` returns historical observations by year, not climate statistics. If agents want to compute percentile-based statistics from historical data, document the proper approach (fetch multi-year data, compute statistics) or recommend against it due to complexity.
- **Pointer:** `SKILL.md`

### What Worked

- The agent correctly identified KTPA as the station for Tampa International Airport
- The agent used `get_single_station_climate_normals` which is the correct function for retrieving climate normal data
- The temperature values (high ~89°F, low ~72°F) and precipitation (~2.6 inches) are reasonable for Tampa in May
- The agent correctly identified that May is relatively dry in Florida and the rainy season starts in June
- The agent provided useful contextual information about the rainy season timing
